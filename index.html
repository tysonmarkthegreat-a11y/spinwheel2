Responsible Gaming

Gambling should be entertainment, not a way to make money or escape problems. Set a budget before you play and never wager more than you can afford to lose. Never chase losses, never gamble while under the influence of alcohol or drugs, and never use funds intended for essentials like rent, bills, or food.

If you feel that gambling is no longer fun, or that it is affecting your finances, relationships, work, or wellbeing, please reach out for free, confidential help:

  • National Council on Problem Gambling (US): 1-800-522-4700, available 24/7
  • Gamblers Anonymous: www.gamblersanonymous.org
  • If you are outside the United States, search for your country's national problem gambling helpline or speak with a local healthcare provider.
